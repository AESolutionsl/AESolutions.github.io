Spiced Garlic Butter Salmon

A golden‑seared salmon fillet basted in garlic, smoked paprika, and brown butter, finished with lemon and fresh herbs. Served over a simple citrus herb rice or roasted vegetables.

Method

Follow the steps; start the timer when you flip the fish.

One non‑stick pan
01
Prep the salmon

Pat the salmon dry with kitchen paper, then season both sides with salt and pepper. Allow it to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.

Room‑temperature fish Better browning
02
Build the spice butter

In a small bowl, mix the butter, garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, lemon zest, and a pinch of salt until you have a soft, fragrant paste.

Garlic butter base
03
Sear the salmon

Heat the olive oil in a non‑stick pan over medium‑high heat. Place the salmon skin‑side down and cook without moving it for 4–5 minutes, until most of the flesh has turned opaque.

Crispy skin Medium‑high heat
04
Flip, baste, and start the timer

Flip the salmon, add the spice butter around the fillets, and baste continuously as it melts and foams. Reduce the heat to medium and cook for another 2–3 minutes. Start the built‑in 10‑minute timer as soon as you flip.

Use timer above Brown butter
05
Rest and finish

Transfer the salmon to a warm plate, spoon over the pan butter, and squeeze over fresh lemon juice. Let the fish rest for 5 minutes before serving, then finish with chopped herbs.

Juicier texture

Cook’s notes

Small changes that make a big difference.

Professional tips
  • Dry salmon thoroughly to encourage a crisp skin and even browning.
  • Browning the butter gently adds nutty flavor; if it darkens too quickly, lower the heat.
  • For meal prep, cool cooked salmon completely before refrigerating in an airtight container.
